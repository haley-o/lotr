The Quest

The Shire

Frodo's journey begins at the Shire. It is here that Frodo has lived all his life, dreaming of an adventure of his own. When Biblo leaves on his 111th birthday the one ring that that forsakened him is now passed onto Frodo, for he is the only one who can carry the burden to Mordor.

Bree

Frodo and Gandalf part ways, On his journey Frodo is joined by Samwise, Pippin and Merry to meet Gandalf at the Prancing Pony at Bree. When the hobbit arrive Gandalf is no where to be seen but instead they are greeted by another, Aragorn. Aragorn helps the hobbits to get to Rivendell.

Rivendell

Frodo is reunited with Gandalf at Rivendell,it is there that he learns that the wizard Sauromon has betrayed Ganfalf. Elrond gathers a council and it is there that the fellowship is created. Frodo will take the ring to Mordor and joining him is Gandalf, Aragorn, Pippin, Merry, Sam, Gimli, Legolas, and Boromir. The fellowship is created and they're quest to take the one ring to Mordor has offically begun.

Mines of Moria

As the fellowship journeys forward they begin experiencing obstacles along their path. Extreme snow on the mountain top causes them to take a new path. While in the mines they discover it has been overtaken by goblins and lurking in the dark is the Balrog. While trying to escape with their lives Gandalf is left behind whilst battling the Balrog.

Lothlorien

The Fellowship continues on, now with only 8. While being hunted by orcs the fellowship goes to Lothlorien, but they are not greeted with the most cheer. With persuassion from Aragorn and Legolas the fellowship stays the night to regain their health and sleep, before they part from Lothlorien they are given farewell gifts from Galadriel.

Amon Hen

The fellowship rows their boats to Amon Hen, it is there they decide to pull ashore and take a rest. The fellowship is then raided by Sauromons orcs. Boromir's life is taken as he defends Merry and Pippin but the hobbits were stolen. Here Frodo decides this quest must be done alone and begins to part ways. Aragorn and Legolas hunt down the orcs in search of Merry and Pippin and Sam chases afrer Frodo.

The Breaking of the Fellowship

This is where this story ends, the fellowship is no longer together but instead are parting ways. Sam finds Frodo and continues to join him on his quest while Aragorn and Legolas hunt down the orcs that have taken Merry and Pippin.

Frodo

Race: Hobbit

Frodo is the child of Drogo Baggins and Primula Brandybuck. When his parents passed away he was taken in by Bilbo Baggins. It was from Bilbo Baggins that he inherited he one ring, having an enormus burden passed along to him, and thus like his uncle Bilbo his own adventure was created.

Sam

Race: Hobbit

Sam, also known as Samwise Gamgee is Frodo's trusty sidekick. Sam has had a vast interest in Elves. He has a kind heart and a rustic love for those closest to him.

Merry and Pippin

Race: Hobbits

Merry and Pippin, also known and Peregrin Took and Meriadoc Brandybuck are close friends as well as cousins. The two are often spotted causing trouble together.

Gandalf

Race: Wizard

Gandalf the grey is one of the last wizards in Middle-earth. Before his quest with Frodo he had helped Frodo's uncle Bilbo and a band of 13 dwarves reclaim their homeland from a dragon. Gandalf takes a lead of the fellowship to help Frodo.

Aragorn

Race: Dunedain

Aragorn, son of Arathorn comes from a race of men known as the Dunedain, who have a longer lifespan. He is also known as strider to others.

Boromir

Race: Human

Boromir is a noble man who comes from Gondor. He is passionate about his people and the greatness of his kingdom and he was caption of the White Tower, and soon later became caption-general.

Legolas

Race: Elvish

Legolas is a Sindarin Elf of the Woodland Realm, his father Thrandiul is the king of Mirkwood, his weapon of choice is a bow and arrow.

Gimli

Race: Dwarf

Gimli, son of Gloin is an honourable, wise and stalwart warrior. His weapon of choice is those similar to axes Gimli's father was one of the company of Thorin Oakenshield (former companions of Bilbo Baggins) to reclaim their homeland.
